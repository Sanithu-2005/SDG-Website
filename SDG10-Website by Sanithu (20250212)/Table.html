This table highlights some of the key global indicators used to track progress toward reducing inequalities. The data points shown here are illustrative global figures from recognized sources.

Selected Indicators for SDG 10 (Reduced Inequalities)
Indicator Definition Baseline Latest 2030 Target Source
Income growth of the bottom 40% Measures the annual income growth rate of the poorest 40% of the population. 1.6% (2015) 2.3% (2023) Should be higher than the national average growth each year. World Bank (PovcalNet)
Gini coefficient Summarizes income inequality (0 = equality, 1 = maximum inequality). 0.39 (2015) 0.36 (2023) Continued reduction from baseline. World Bank, WIID Database
Social protection coverage Population covered by at least one social protection benefit. 48% (2015) 57% (2024) At least 70% coverage by 2030. International Labour Organization (ILO)
Remittance transfer costs Average cost of sending $200 internationally, as a percentage. 7.0% (2015) 5.0% (2024) 3% or less, with no corridors above 5%. World Bank, Remittance Prices Worldwide
People reporting discrimination Percentage of people reporting discrimination or harassment in the past year. 21% (2016) 17% (2023) Ongoing decline with stronger legal protection. UN SDG Global Database
Labour income share of GDP Portion of GDP going to workers as wages and labour income. 53% (2015) 55% (2024) Maintain or increase relative to baseline. ILO, ILOSTAT
Figures are based on globally available data. National values may differ depending on context.
